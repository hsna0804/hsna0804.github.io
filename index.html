About Me


Hi! I am Sue.

I began my career as a scientist, driven by a deep curiosity about microbes—tiny, invisible organisms that are everywhere and incredibly powerful. My passion for science led me to leave my home country, South Korea, and pursue opportunities across the globe.

Along the way, I discovered that my true strength lies in bringing structure to chaos, communicating science clearly, and collaborating with people from diverse backgrounds. With the support of inspiring senior program managers who mentored me selflessly, and project sponsors who took a chance on me, I successfully transitioned into project management.


I carry with me the inspiration and generosity that others showed me. My goal is to pay that forward—to become a source of inspiration for others and to help more people recognize the critical role of project management in turning great ideas into successful outcomes.



As a Project Manager ..

I'm a Project Manager passionate about bringing interdisciplinary teams together to tackle complex challenges. I lead by fostering clarity and alignment, which helps teams stay focused in fast-paced corporate environments where priorities and strategies can shift quickly.

With a strong technical background, I bridge the gap between technical and commercial teams. I understand the challenges engineers face and ensure risks are identified early and clearly communicated. I take ownership of the project's "why" and work to keep everyone aligned toward a shared goal.

Typical Project Examples

Product Development (Feasibility)

  • Phase 1: Feasibility
  • Goal: Develop a scalable MVP for early market entry.

Typical deliverables at the end of the phase (stage gate review)

  • 1. Commercial feasibility - Business case
    • Value proposition
    • Functional & non-functional requirements
    • Target economics
  • 2. Technical feasibility
    • Prototype that meets minimum performance requirements
    • Preliminary manufacturing cost estimate
  • 3. Operational feasibility
    • Knowns/assumptions for regulatory requirements and product certification
    • Budget & Resource allocation
  • 4. Project charter
    • Objectives, Scope, Deliverables
    • Schedule (Milestone, performance metrics)
    • Budget
    • High-level risks & mitigation plans

New Product Introduction (NPI)

  • Phase 2: Customer Development
  • Goal: Secure offtake or development deals with customers.
    (usually to secure a certain volume of sales at a certain price, to prepare the product for scale-up and commercialization in the near future)

Typical deliverables at the end of the phase (stage gate review)

  • 1. Business plan
    • Offtake or development agreements signed
  • 2. MVP package
    • Techno-economic model (a process that meets target economics)
    • Preliminary manufacturing specs based on successful production of product at (pilot) scale.
    • Preliminary product specs based on sample evaluation and customer feedback.
  • 3. Regulatory/IP strategy
